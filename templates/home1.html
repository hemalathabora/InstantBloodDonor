Blood Donation Eligibility

Here are the eligibility guidelines for blood donation,You are considered a perfect blood donor if you meet all of the conditions listed below.

1. Age: Donors must typically be at least 17 years old (age requirements may vary).

2. Weight: Donors should weigh at least 110 pounds (50 kilograms).

3. Health: Donors should be in good health on the day of donation, without any acute or chronic illnesses.

4. Travel: Recent travel to high-risk areas may affect eligibility.

5. Medications: Some medications can impact eligibility; inform the center about any medications.

6. Blood Pressure: Blood pressure should be within a certain range.

7. Iron Levels: Adequate iron levels are required.

8. Pregnancy: Pregnant individuals are generally not eligible to donate.

9. Medical History: Detailed medical history questions will be asked.

10. High-Risk Behaviors: Engagement in high-risk behaviors may disqualify donors.

Why Should I Donate Blood?

Donating blood is a critical and selfless act that can save lives and provide numerous benefits to both the recipients and the donors themselves. Donating blood can save lives in emergencies, surgeries, and medical treatments. Provides a sense of satisfaction and fulfillment.
